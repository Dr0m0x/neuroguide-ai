Abstract

Artificial intelligence has become increasingly capable of supporting healthcare education through natural language processing, machine learning, and knowledge retrieval techniques. However, general-purpose large language models frequently generate inaccurate or unsupported medical information because they rely on static training data and lack mechanisms for validating responses against authoritative sources. These limitations are particularly concerning within cognitive health, where patients and caregivers require reliable educational information regarding Alzheimer's disease, mild cognitive impairment, dementia, genetics, and lifestyle interventions.

This project proposes the development of NeuroGuide AI, an explainable multi-agent artificial intelligence platform that combines Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG), neural networks, explainable artificial intelligence (XAI), and specialized AI agents to deliver evidence-based cognitive health education. Unlike traditional chatbots, NeuroGuide AI will coordinate multiple autonomous agents responsible for document retrieval, clinical reasoning, fact verification, personalization, and response generation. The platform will also incorporate a neural network trained on publicly available Alzheimer's datasets to provide educational risk assessments while utilizing SHAP explainability techniques to improve transparency.

The project will employ quantitative and qualitative evaluation methodologies including RAGAS metrics, usability testing, System Usability Scale (SUS) surveys, response latency measurements, expert review, and comparative experiments against baseline single-agent RAG systems. The research seeks to demonstrate that combining explainable AI with multi-agent retrieval architectures improves factual accuracy, user trust, and educational value while reducing hallucinations.

Keywords: Artificial Intelligence, Retrieval-Augmented Generation, Multi-Agent Systems, Neural Networks, Explainable AI, Alzheimer's Disease, Cognitive Health, Large Language Models

Proposal
Project Title

NeuroGuide AI: An Explainable Multi-Agent Artificial Intelligence Platform for Personalized Cognitive Health Education Using Neural Networks and Retrieval-Augmented Generation

Statement of the Project

The rapid advancement of large language models has transformed how users access medical information. Despite these advancements, current conversational AI systems remain susceptible to hallucinations, outdated knowledge, and limited transparency. These shortcomings present significant risks within healthcare, where inaccurate information may negatively influence user decision-making.

This capstone project proposes the development of NeuroGuide AI, an intelligent educational platform designed specifically for cognitive health education. Rather than functioning as a traditional chatbot, the system will integrate multiple artificial intelligence techniques learned throughout the Master of Science in Artificial Intelligence curriculum.

The platform will combine Retrieval-Augmented Generation with specialized AI agents capable of independently retrieving scientific literature, validating evidence, reasoning across multiple sources, and producing user-friendly educational responses. Additionally, the platform will incorporate a neural network trained on publicly available Alzheimer's disease datasets to estimate educational risk categories based on user-provided demographic and lifestyle information. These predictions will not provide medical diagnoses but instead demonstrate explainable machine learning through SHAP feature attribution.

Furthermore, incorporating explainable neural network predictions through SHAP will improve user understanding of AI-generated recommendations while maintaining ethical transparency.

Proposed System Architecture

The proposed architecture consists of five collaborating AI agents.

Agent 1 — Retrieval Agent

Retrieves evidence from PubMed, NIH, WHO, MedlinePlus, Alzheimer's Association, and peer-reviewed literature.

Agent 2 — Clinical Reasoning Agent

Synthesizes retrieved evidence into coherent explanations.

Agent 3 — Validation Agent

Cross-checks generated responses against retrieved evidence while identifying unsupported claims.

Agent 4 — Personalization Agent

Produces educational recommendations based on user characteristics including age, APOE status, family history, exercise, sleep, and cardiovascular risk factors.

Agent 5 — Communication Agent

Rewrites responses for multiple audiences including patients, caregivers, students, and clinicians.

Ethical Considerations

The project recognizes several ethical challenges associated with healthcare artificial intelligence.

The platform will clearly identify itself as an educational system rather than a diagnostic tool. Every response will include appropriate medical disclaimers encouraging users to consult licensed healthcare professionals for clinical decisions.

Bias mitigation strategies will include evaluating responses across diverse demographic groups and ensuring that retrieved information originates from authoritative healthcare organizations.

No protected health information will be collected or permanently stored.

All generated responses will provide citations to the retrieved evidence to promote transparency and user trust.

Explainable AI techniques will provide users with understandable explanations of neural network predictions rather than opaque probability scores.
